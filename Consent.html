Key Information

Title of research study: Evaluating Trivia Statements

This consent form asks you to take part in a research study. The study is being conducted by Dr. Elizabeth Marsh, Ph.D. at Duke University.

Purpose: To study how people evaluate statements that reflect general knowledge. What you will be asked to do: This research study consists of one session. In this experiment, you may rate read and rate trivia statements. This session should take about 60 minutes to complete. At the end of the study you will be paid $8.00, but only if you finish the entire study. Participation in this study is voluntary. You will be compensated after the end of the session. To stop, click on the “Return HIT” button, or close your browser window.

How your confidentiality will be maintained: If you choose to participate, we will not ask your name. However, we will collect your Amazon Mechanical Turk worker identification number for payment purposes and record keeping. We will collect your worker ID on the next page before you begin the study. All data will be securely saved at all times. All data we keep will be de-identified, indefinitely.

Benefits and Risks: This research will not benefit you personally. The researcher will be happy to discuss the details of the study with you in detail at its completion. We know of no risks to you for participating in this study.

If you have any questions about this research, please ask the experimenter who set up this “HIT”. You may also contact the Dr. Elizabeth Marsh (marshlab@duke.edu) if you have any questions about the study. You may contact the Duke University Campus Institutional Review Board (919)-684-3030 or campusirb@duke.edu) about your rights as a research participant.

Clicking Accept: By clicking on the “Accept HIT” button, you indicate that you are 18 years of age or older, that you voluntarily agree to participate in this study and that you understand the information in this consent form. You have not waived any legal rights you otherwise would have as a participant in a research study.
